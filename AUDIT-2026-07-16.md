**Third-party audit of the Epistemic Debt program, commissioned by the owner.**

## 1. Overall verdict

This is an unusually honest research program with an unusually strong evidentiary
core and an unusually weak packaging layer.

- **Every number any auditor recomputed from primary artifacts matched its reported
  value** — across v4, v5, transfer, manifest, coarseness, reasoning-reader, curve,
  routing, budgetline (both parsers), highpower (including every Wilson CI), domains,
  rescore, realdoc, certificates, sections, forced-CoT, distill-parity, the clamped
  campaign, and 12 exact counts across 8 proof scripts. Zero fabricated or drifting
  numbers were found anywhere.
- **95/95 tests pass.** Every `THEOREM` label in ledger rows 14–24 rests on a genuine
  written proof (not finite checking alone); `EXACT` claims are exhaustive within
  honestly printed scope boxes; classical specializations are labeled classical
  *without being asked* (Plott 1973, view determinacy, decision-tree certificate
  complexity).
- **Refutations are reported as refutations**, VOID labels are carried honestly on
  every prose surface, and the parser-artifact self-correction loop (rows 20/39/40)
  is real — one auditor re-ran the corrected parse independently and confirmed the
  load-bearing conjuncts survive.
- Total spend **$32.24** reconstructs bottom-up to within ~1–2%.

The defects are concentrated in three layers, none of them scientific fabrication:

1. **Numerical surface errors** — ~16 confirmed wrong numbers in prose surfaces
   (none verdict-changing; all listed in §4). A program whose credibility *is* its
   self-audit cannot leave numbers its own artifacts refute in its most-cited law.
2. **Provenance gaps** — the foundational PREREGISTERED result (v4) has no prereg
   artifact; several campaigns froze prereg and results in a single commit; the
   newest third of the ledger (6 proof rows + reference benchmark) exists only in
   one uncommitted working tree.
3. **Strategic lag** — the theory doc, positioning doc, README, site, and queue are
   each frozen at a different date, and two pieces of concurrent work (one of them
   the strongest competitor found anywhere) are unintegrated. The priority window
   on the flagship claim is months, not years.

**Bottom line: the science is real; the moat is credibility; the risk is that the
program's own surfaces are currently its worst enemy.**

## 2. The diamond in the rough

**The program has accidentally built a measurement standard the industry will be
forced to adopt — and it is presenting it as a table row.**

The diamond is the **justified-accuracy mismeasurement thesis**, and its strongest
single exhibit:

> On real NTSB prose (row 31), blind compaction destroys every witness; **decision
> accuracy reads 0.87–1.00 while justified accuracy J = 0.00–0.07**. The metric
> every pipeline reports cannot see the collapse; the metric nobody reports sees
> it exactly.

Why this is the diamond and not the (excellent) alternatives:

- **Evidence depth is unmatched**: dissociation preregistered across **4 vendor
  families**, N=400 replication, 6 document schemas, real prose, a quantitative law
  (J ≥ S, affine fit R² = 0.96), and a distillation-side echo (Δ_V 0.980 vs Δ_J
  0.686 while the verdict-only student *wins* the in-domain gauge — invisible to
  accuracy, visible to Δ).
- **It is the demand-side asset**: the loss-ledger router, fusion contract,
  certificates, and honesty training are all *supply-side* fixes. The meter is what
  makes the industry need them. Every deployed agent performs exactly the
  compression that triggers this failure; no leaderboard, model card, or regulation
  currently measures it.
- **The missing work is the cheapest kind**: packaging and replication, not
  discovery. (a) Ship the meter as a versioned public benchmark + CLI;
  (b) replicate the realdoc demo at high power across ≥3 natural domains;
  (c) run the candidate-blind and valence add-back experiments that defend the
  mechanism against arXiv:2606.29251. Priority window: **months** — the converging
  literature (2606.29251, Knowledge Objects, Kwon 2606.25449, the RLM ecosystem) is
  absorbing the framing now.

### The theorem that turns the diamond into a field

The program's hardest empirical result — no reader, of any vendor or reasoning
tier, ever recovers a witness the compactor destroyed — **is one-way communication
complexity, and nobody says so.** The transfer grid + reasoning-reader results are
data-processing for the compactor→reader protocol, re-proven empirically four times
without being named. The missing theorem:

> **The honesty tax is the gap between the one-way communication complexity of
> "answer" and of "answer-or-certified-abstain"** — lower-bounded by a task-geometry
> quantity computable from the accepted-output cover curve Γ_q(C).

The machinery already exists (Γ_q row 22, the access-tradeoff bound
`b + t·w ≥ ⌈log₂(N−F−E)⌉` row 42, the partition search). Proving it converts Law 2
from an accounting identity plus measured multiples into the field's first rate
theorem, answers the IB-triviality objection with mathematics instead of
positioning, and gives the honesty premium its missing necessity direction.

### The boldest claim the data cannot yet rule out

> **Designed-in honesty is generically *cheaper* than the debt it removes** — the
> 0.47× premium on the realistic causal_referee is the rule, and the 2.3–7.4×
> synthetic premiums are the engineered worst case.

Current data: 1 of 4 families below 1, crossing at exactly one budget. Nothing in
the repo even conjectures the sign of the generic case, and two "honesty can be
cheap" results (the premium dichotomy; the certificate-quotient shortcut) rest on
**unreconciled mechanisms** — causal_referee has *no* certificate shortcut, so its
cheap honesty is not the certificate cheapness. Identifying the task invariant that
predicts premium/D < 1 vs > 1 is a near-free exact computation over existing
frontier tables, and the payoff is a better headline than anything currently on
the site.

## 3. What you're missing (the gaps that re-price everything)

### 3.1 The existential experiment: production-scale budgets (~$5–15)

**Every one of the 42 results lives at artifact budgets ≤ 80 words over sources ≤
~500 words.** The program's own exchange-rate curve puts the reason-channel
midpoint at ≈30 realized words and J at ceiling by ~60 words — meaning **the
program's own data predicts the shelf closes in the regime where production
compaction operates** (10⁴–10⁵-token sources → 10²–10³-token summaries). If the
shelf closes at production budgets, 42 rows are a small-budget curiosity; if it
persists (e.g., witnesses displaced at any compression *ratio*), the program
graduates. Nothing — no design, no cost line, no queue entry — tests this. It is
the only missing experiment whose outcome re-prices the entire ledger at once, in
either direction, for roughly a third of all-time spend. Design sketch: NTSB full
narratives + concatenated corpora (5k–50k tokens), artifact budgets 100–4,000
tokens, fixed compression *ratio* arms, standard decision+WHICH+Δ battery.

### 3.2 The missing mathematics

1. **The honesty-tax lower bound** (§2) — the unifying theorem. $0.
2. **The premium-dichotomy invariant** — enumerate invariant candidates (certificate
   multiplicity, answer-class separability, fiber entanglement) over the existing
   frontier tables. $0.
3. **The distributional turn** — all exact results are worst-case/uniform; Law 1's
   rate-distortion target [R_A(0), R_W(0)) is *stated but never computed* even on
   Q_(k,p) families where it is a finite stdlib computation. This is also the
   explicit blocker on the repaired Shelf Width Law.
4. **Economics with actual content** — the doc says "market," "mechanism design,"
   and "sells justification first" with zero formal objects (no agent, objective,
   equilibrium). The material is sitting unused: the shelf is a *pooling
   equilibrium* (answer evals can't separate justified from unjustified systems);
   the fusion instruction-hierarchy result is a *costly signal* (models pay
   1.7–1.9× realized length to keep witnesses); the abstention probe is a
   *screening device*. Enabling theorem: **Δ-mandates break the pooling equilibrium
   iff the honesty premium is below the retrofit gap at the mandated budget** —
   evaluable from the existing tables today. Either build this or strip the
   vocabulary; a reviewer will ask for the equilibrium.
5. **Justification logic (Artemov) and AGM belief revision** — the fusion contract
   `claim + deciding-value + pointer` *is* a justification term `t:φ`; the
   versioned-memory contract *is* belief revision with versions. For a program
   named "epistemic debt," silence here reads as naiveté to a logic reviewer. Cite,
   don't build — cheap to add, expensive to omit.

## 5. Structural weaknesses (systemic, not numeric)

1. **Preregistration provenance is the program's soft underbelly.** The
   foundational PREREGISTERED rows (26–31, v4) have **no prereg artifact at all** —
   P-A…P-D live only inside the runner that computes the results; every wave-1 file
   shares one mtime in one commit. realdoc and certificates froze prereg and
   results in the same commit. Two frozen campaign criteria (v5 "≥3 of 4 models";
   transfer "≥10 of 12 pairs") became unreachable and were silently adapted.
   Mitigants: predictions failed informatively; later campaigns (distill-parity,
   sections, forced-CoT) set the correct standard — standalone freeze commits plus
   threshold-pinning tests. **Adopt that standard universally and retro-document
   v4.** The label PREREGISTERED is the program's entire currency.
2. **The newest third of the evidence chain exists only in one working tree.**
   23+ untracked/uncommitted files: 5 proofs + 5 frozen JSONs + the boundary test +
   boundary report + the entire infinite-context reference benchmark + RESULTS.md
   rows 14–24. 95-green cannot be reproduced from a fresh clone; CI has never seen
   the changeset. **Commit and push now** — this is unhedged single-laptop risk on
   the strongest part of the ledger.
3. **Statistical machinery gaps.** No multiplicity policy across ~100+ frozen
   pass/fail evaluations (grep: zero hits for Bonferroni/Holm/BH/FDR); thresholds
   preregistered but underived, with no operating-characteristics analysis (P-L1
   passes ~1% of the time at n=30 under a true constant-gap law — the one pass is
   genuinely fragile); the within-item clustering caveat is applied to exactly one
   campaign family while realdoc (12 documents × 5 variants), curve/budgetline
   (30 items × 7 budgets), transfer (shared items across 9 cells), and sections
   share the same structure unflagged; and the dissociation's **paired structure is
   underexploited** — McNemar exact on v4's lost cell gives **p = 0.0018**, far
   stronger than the "non-overlapping CIs" framing. The program systematically

4. **The machine-readable layer drifts from the prose layer.** Results JSONs still

   5–7 results JSONs lack the anomaly/UNMATCHED fields the repo's own ground rule
   mandates; highpower/domains ship no `scored.csv`. Add `superseded_by`/`void`
   fields and enforce the anomaly rule in `tests/`.
5. **The settlement re-analysis exists nowhere as code.** An EXACT row, a deflated
   law, and a successor campaign's frozen predictors rest on an analysis with no
   checked-in script and no test (its artifact link points to a README containing

6. **The theory doc is a palimpsest.** §5–§8 froze ~2026-07-08: its kill-shot list
