# Exact boundary of infinite, non-degrading justified context

**Date:** 2026-07-09  
**Scope:** internal research report; no public significance claim  
**Overall status:** **STRONG SPECIALIZED THEOREM + NEGATIVE BOUNDARY**  
**Program level after this audit:** Level 3, not Level 4

## Executive verdict

The current package cannot honestly be promoted to a new, architecture-
independent general theory of infinite non-degrading context.

It does contain a sharp positive class, under an explicit fixed ontology and
authenticated-summary trust boundary:

> **THEOREM — causal sparse temporal refinement.** For a fixed finite cell and
> value ontology with immutable append-only publications, \(r(n)=o(n)\)
> complete-frontier checkpoints arriving exactly at their boundaries under a
> free exogenous schedule can be maintained with \(o(n)\) active information,
> zero archive reads at declaration, \(O(m)\) refinement work per declaration,
> and zero answer or complete-frontier error. A standalone exact last-\(w\)
> window component adds \(O(\log n+w)\) bits and answers retroactive boundaries
> inside the window with at most \(w\) query-time replays. Combined with the
> checkpoints, total active information is
> \(O(r+r\log(1+n/r)+\log n+w)\). Targeted invalidation of an earlier
> publication is excluded. Local verification of completeness still relies on
> the authenticated-summary primitive.

It also contains a sharp negative class:

> **THEOREM — counterexample-support hitting.** Relative to a fixed visible
> state, every perfectly sound and complete raw-archive proof system must, on
> each accepted proof, probe cells hitting every still-indistinguishable
> archive modification that falsifies its claimed output. In the finite model
> with unbounded proof length and computation, the minimum accepted-proof probe
> count is exactly the minimum archive cylinder whose intersection with that
> visible fiber excludes every falsifier.
> Complete negative evidence can therefore require \(n\) probes when all
> singleton falsifying flips remain in that visible fiber, even though the
> answer is one bit, while a positive witness needs one.

Those results do not combine into a foundational new theory. The semantic law
for archive dormancy is classical **view determinacy**; update-side dormancy is
classical **view self-maintainability**; relational certificate state is
classical incomplete-FSM compatible-block/closed-cover theory; proof-assisted
archive access already has nondeterministic cell-probe and annotated-streaming
models. The new exact formulas remain credible specialized novelty candidates,
but a general Level-4 novelty claim would currently be false.

The package also required four corrections before extension:

1. the reported \(34/55/209\) two-sided counts are for the full formal raw
   monoid, not the submonoid generated by the stated depth-one event alphabet;
   the generated counts are \(32/53/206\);
2. the add-only \(p+1\) count is a fixed-priority **certificate** count, not an
   answer-continuation count; answer-only add-union uses 2 states and deletion
   forces \(2^p\);
3. the dynamic rows are exact-length layer widths with a free horizon clock;
   a uniform no-free-clock current machine through horizon four has 886 states,
   not 579;
4. the versioned checker recomputes complete frontiers from raw history and
   does not implement a local authenticated completeness proof.

These are scope/label corrections, not failures of the surviving finite
theorems.

## II. General exact search framework

The new finite engine represents a contract by:

\[
(H,\Sigma,s_0,O,A,T_L),
\]

where \(H\) is a finite semantic state set, \(\Sigma\) is an update alphabet,
\(O\) is a set of accepted-output labels, \(A(h)\subseteq O\) is the nonempty
accepted-output relation, and optional \(T_L\) supplies a coherent left action
that commutes with the declared right action. Without such a left action,
two-sided quantities are `INAPPLICABLE`; they are not inferred from right
transitions alone.

The optimized implementation enumerates:

- answer-compatible and complete-output-compatible blocks;
- all minimum static partitions;
- all minimum right- and two-sided-congruent partitions;
- compatible covers;
- transition-closed covers with canonical transition witnesses;
- accepted-output-labelled closed covers.

The independent implementation does not enumerate partitions or covers. It
enumerates BFS-canonical deterministic output-labelled machines,
product-simulates each with the semantic transition system, and verifies every
reachable semantic/machine pair. This includes path-dependent machines in
which one semantic state is reached with different hidden path states. On the
frozen fixtures it independently checks the minimum closed-cover/direct-machine
counts; the optimized engine's complete partition, congruence, block, and
transition listings are not all independently reproduced.

Exact fixture results:

| Fixture | Static output partition | Right output partition | Closed output cover | Direct machine |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Shareable proof blocks ({0,1},{1,2}) | 2 | 3 | 2 | 2 |
| State/version-bound proofs | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| Three-state commutative static/right gap | 2 | 3 | 3 | 3 |

The framework is deterministic, canonical, stdlib-only, and cross-checks the
fixture minima. Current safe generic limits are semantic state count about 6
for cover search and machine count about 4 for small alphabets. It is a partial
finite contract generator: this pass did not automate every requested semantic
axis (versions, revisions, negative evidence, and ontology changes are instead
represented by dedicated exact families), and accepted-output labels by
themselves do not measure proof size or local verification work.

## IV. The strongest generalized phase law

### 1. Arbitrary contracts: continuation-index growth is complete but classical

For a fixed contract family \(K_n\), the exact active information of a
deterministic semantic-state quotient is the logarithm of its minimum
admissible right-congruence count. If path-dependent hidden machine states are
allowed, a transition-closed accepted-output cover can be smaller and is a
different optimization problem. Either growth can be \(O(1)\), \(O(\log n)\),
\(o(n)\), \(\Theta(n)\), or identity-like.
Without restrictions on the accepted-output relation, essentially arbitrary
distinction geometries can be encoded. No simpler obligation-count or density
statistic can characterize every contract.

That is the honest architecture-independent answer, but it is state complexity,
not a new quantity.

### 2. Linear contracts: exact rank classification

Fix a horizon \(n\). Let histories be \(x\in\mathrm{GF}(2)^n\) and let the
unique complete contract output be \(A_nx\). Then:

> **THEOREM L1.** The exact fixed-horizon quotient has
> \(2^{\operatorname{rank}A_n}\) states and requires exactly
> \(\operatorname{rank}A_n\) bits.

Proof: two histories merge exactly when their difference lies in
\(\ker A_n\). The quotient therefore has
\(2^n/2^{n-\operatorname{rank}A_n}\) cosets.

For a fixed-horizon extension from rows \(A\) to rows
\(\begin{bmatrix}A\\B\end{bmatrix}\), when the retained old state is **exactly**
\(Ax\) and contains no uncharged auxiliary information:

\[
\text{archive-dormant extension}
\iff
\operatorname{row}(B)\subseteq\operatorname{row}(A),
\]

and the constrained refinement tag needs exactly

\[
\operatorname{rank}\!\begin{bmatrix}A\\B\end{bmatrix}
-\operatorname{rank}(A)
\]

bits. This is not a lower bound for implementations that deliberately retained
additional auxiliary sketches before the extension.

This supplies an exact partial classification:

- one global parity: 1 bit;
- power-of-two point obligations: \(\Theta(\log n)\) bits;
- square-index points: \(\Theta(\sqrt n)=o(n)\) bits;
- all length-three windows: \(n-2\) bits;
- all prefixes, all intervals, or all points: \(n\) bits.

The implementation independently compares Gaussian rank with exhaustive output
signature counts. This is a fixed-horizon classification. It becomes an online
machine theorem only for a cross-horizon family whose retained syndromes admit
compatible updates (and after stating whether the horizon clock is free). It
generalizes points, intervals, windows, and linear causal incidences, but it is
classical linear algebra and must be labelled **STRONG SPECIALIZED THEOREM /
CLASSICAL METHOD**.

### 3. Complete-frontier temporal contracts: exact segment law

For fixed \(m\) cells and \(v\) values, let \(G_{m,v}(g)\) count one gap’s exact
per-cell increment/latest-value summaries. At exogenous checkpoints
\(0=t_0<\cdots<t_r=n\):

\[
N_T(n)=\prod_{j=1}^{r}G_{m,v}(t_j-t_{j-1}).
\]

For fixed \(m,v\),

\[
\log N_T(n)
=O\!\left(r+r\log(1+n/r)\right).
\]

Thus \(r=o(n)\) implies \(o(n)\) active information. If the event alphabet is
nontrivial (in the present family, \(mv>1\)), positive-density independent gaps
give linear information, and the unrestricted retroactive
boundary family is identity-like. This is the strongest exact non-linear
specialized phase law in the package.

## V. Evolving contracts

### Formalization

Let \(K_0\preceq K_1\) be a conservative extension over the same history/update
system: old answers remain unchanged; old accepted certificates may be
strengthened; new queries may be added. Semantic migration that changes the
event alphabet or meanings of old answers is a separate operation.

Let \(s_0:H\to S_0\) be the old machine and \(B_s=s_0^{-1}(s)\). For a
single-valued new residual behavior \(\rho_1\), define

\[
r_s=|\{\rho_1(h):h\in B_s\}|.
\]

If migration must retain the old state identifier and add a refinement tag,
then the constrained refined state count is

\[
N_{\mathrm{ref}}=\sum_s r_s,
\qquad
D_{\mathrm{split}}=\sum_s(r_s-1),
\qquad
b_{\mathrm{tag}}\ge\max_s\lceil\log_2r_s\rceil.
\]

For relational certificates, the functional formula does not decompose by
independently optimizing each old fiber. The refinement must be one **global**
right-closed partition or one global transition-closed cover that refines the
old-state map; successor choices couple blocks across old fibers. The sum
formula applies only after such a globally valid functional residual map has
been fixed.

The exact versioned split profiles show why one global ratio is insufficient:

- \(m=2,v=2,n=3\), current \(12\to32\) with a new boundary at 1:
  4 old states split into 2 and 8 split into 3;
- \(m=4,v=3,n=3\), current \(228\to792\): 120 old states split into 3
  and 108 split into 4.

### Dormancy is view determinacy

For a functional new query \(q\), zero archive access and zero state refinement
are possible exactly when \(q\) factors through the old view/state:

\[
s_0(h)=s_0(h')\Longrightarrow q(h)=q(h').
\]

For relational complete outputs, every old fiber must have a common accepted
output. Online support additionally requires one globally coherent right-closed
machine or transition-closed cover after every continuation. This is the
contract version of information-theoretic view
determinacy, studied directly by Nash, Segoufin, and Vianu in
[Views and Queries: Determinacy and Rewriting](https://doi.org/10.1145/1806907.1806913).
It is exact but classical. For rich conjunctive-query languages, even deciding
determinacy is undecidable ([Gogacz–Marcinkowski 2015](https://doi.org/10.1109/LICS.2015.35)).

Update-side archive dormancy is the materialized-view self-maintainability
problem. Blakeley, Coburn, and Larson give necessary and sufficient conditions
for a relational view to be updated without base access
([1988](https://doi.org/10.1016/0306-4379(88)90005-1)).

### Positive structured extension classes

#### Causal checkpoints

For immutable append-only publications, maintain the live segment summary since
the last boundary. Under a fixed exogenous/free schedule, a checkpoint signal
arrives exactly at its boundary; freeze that summary and start an empty segment.
If deadlines are announced earlier and are not supplied again by an external
scheduler, their pending schedule metadata must also be retained and is not
priced here. Targeted invalidation of an earlier publication is excluded. The
executable checks 82,453 history/schedule pairs against direct prefix replay.

Resource profile for fixed \(m,v\), \(r=o(n)\), counting one queried complete
frontier as the certificate and treating its authenticated integrity as a
primitive:

\[
(B,A,P,U,F,E)
=
(o(n),0,O(m\log n)\text{ per queried frontier},
 O(m)\text{ per declaration},0,0).
\]

This is the strongest positive theorem in this report.

#### Bounded retroactivity

Maintain an exact frontier at \(n-w\) plus the exact last \(w\) immutable
publication events. Every new boundary \(\tau\in[n-w,n]\) is answered by at
most \(w\) retained-event replays:

\[
B=O(\log n+w),\qquad W_{\rm query}\le w,\qquad A=F=E=0.
\]

The replay is query work; the executable does not persist it as a refined
state. For \(w=o(n)\), the standalone window component and its worst-case query
replay are sublinear. Combined with \(r\) causal checkpoints, active information
is \(O(r+r\log(1+n/r)+\log n+w)\). An AB/BA pair before the window, followed by
an identical suffix, proves that an older boundary is not determined in
general.

#### Stable-key indexed additions

The exact benchmark demonstrates a systems candidate: maintain a complete
stable-key posting directory at ingestion, charge every index write, and refine
only the newly requested keys. A new key with \(d\) postings costs \(O(d)\)
indexed event reads rather than a full \(n\)-event replay. A production theorem
would need a fixed authenticated-dictionary model, word size, update cost,
nonmembership proof, and matching lower bound. Persistent authenticated
dictionaries already support historical membership queries
([Anagnostopoulos–Goodrich–Tamassia](https://doi.org/10.1007/3-540-45439-X_26)),
so the component architecture is not novel.

### Refinement work is conditional decision-tree complexity

Two extensions can split every old fiber into the same number of new states but
need radically different raw-archive work. Over an \(n\)-bit old fiber, asking
for bit 1 costs one direct probe; asking for parity still has two outputs but
requires all \(n\) raw probes without an index. The appropriate exact object is
the conditional archive decision-tree complexity of the new compatible
refinement, relative to a fixed archive layout and retained indices.

Old proofs also require two invalidation counters:

- \(I_{\mathrm{false}}\): proof no longer valid because old semantics/trust
  changed;
- \(I_{\mathrm{incomplete}}\): proof remains valid for \(K_0\) but does not
  discharge new \(K_1\) obligations.

A conservative query addition normally increases the second, not the first.

## VI. Proof-carrying closed-cover verdict

The universal collapse conjecture is refuted only at the abstract
accepted-output-relation level. The requested verdict for locally checkable,
resource-priced proof protocols remains open.

> **EXACT accepted-output witness.** With validity supports
> \(V_\alpha=\{0,1\}\) and \(V_\beta=\{1,2\}\), the visible two-state path
> machine selects one accepted proof label per state, its transitions preserve block
> inclusion, and every reachable semantic/machine pair satisfies the chosen
> proof. Every semantic partition requires three states.

> **EXACT negative witness.** If the same apparent proof names are bound to
> exact semantic identities—`alpha@0`, `alpha@1`, `beta@1`, `beta@2`—neither
> overlap has a common proof and the minimum returns to three.

Therefore, at this abstract level:

- atomic accepted-output labels do **not** universally erase overlap;
- semantic overlap does **not** guarantee a provenance-valid local proof;
- the correct object is a Pareto frontier over visible/path state, proof bits,
  verifier work/probes, archive dependence, update closure, and refinement.

The finite cover engine proves only the accepted-output relation part of that
statement. It treats labels as atomic: it does not encode a proof string,
verifier, local transcript, or proof-size/work budget. The benchmark's
state/version-bound labels demonstrate one provenance-sensitive instance, and
the hitting theorem prices raw-cell verification separately. A canonical
minimal **locally verified** proof-carrying envelope remains open.

Accordingly, this deliverable is a **partial negative verdict**, not a solved
locally verified envelope. This is classical weighted
incomplete-FSM/verification territory until a new
coupling theorem is proved. Paull and Unger’s 1959 incomplete-machine work is a
direct ancestor ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1109/TEC.1959.5222697)).

## VII. Unified resource laws: strongest honest form

### Accepted-output cover curve

For a fixed query \(q\), let \(O_q(h)\) be the nonempty set of accepted complete
outputs on history \(h\). For each output \(y\), define its validity fiber

\[
A_y=\{h:y\in O_q(h)\}.
\]

Define the exact fast-coverage curve

\[
\Gamma_q(C)
=
\max_{|Y|\le C}\left|\bigcup_{y\in Y}A_y\right|.
\]

Equivalently, let \(\kappa_q(S)\) be the minimum number of globally compatible
blocks covering \(S\); then
\(\Gamma_q(C)=\max\{|S|:\kappa_q(S)\le C\}\).

For the fixed deterministic query below, (F) is the number of histories
routed to fallback, and (E) is the number of **nonfallback** histories on
which the emitted output is invalid. Thus the two sets are disjoint; these are
cardinalities, not expected or amortized quantities.

> **THEOREM R1.** A deterministic fast path with \(b\) active bits and at most
> \(t\) adaptive \(w\)-bit archive reads satisfies
>
> \[
> N-F-E\le\Gamma_q\!\left(2^{b+tw}\right).
> \]

Proof: every correct nonfallback transcript emits one output, so its history
fiber lies inside that output’s validity fiber. Addresses contain no additional
information because they are deterministic functions of the query, state, and
earlier contents.

Consequences:

- history-identifying outputs recover \(b+tw\ge\log_2(N-F-E)\);
- noninjective single-valued answers depend on image size rather than history
  count;
- overlapping certificates can cover more histories per transcript;
- a **proof-component-size eligibility** budget restricts outputs by the
  declared `proof_bits` field before computing \(\Gamma\); the executable does
  not price answer encoding or verifier work;
  proof bits generated by the responder are not free
  incoming information.

The exact engine cross-checks 646 acceptance hypergraphs by combination search
and subset dynamic programming. It also checks a proof-component-size
eligibility example in which one unrestricted transcript covers all 6 histories
but restricting eligible proof components to one bit covers at most 3.

### Static simultaneous-query frontier

Alternative queries cannot have their capacities multiplied as though all were
run. For per-query capacities \(C_q\), define

\[
m_K(\{C_q\})
=
\min_{\mathcal P}|\mathcal P|
\quad\text{s.t.}\quad
\kappa_q(B)\le C_q
\text{ for every }B\in\mathcal P,q.
\]

Any exact deterministic semantic-state partition satisfies

\[
2^b\ge m_K(\{2^{t_qw}\}).
\]

This is a precise finite **static, partition-based, cover-relaxed necessary
bound**. The executable's nontrivial fixture uses two crossing binary answer
partitions: either query alone needs two no-read states, while one common
partition needs four. The executable does not prove that the
minimizing partition is right-stable under updates, nor that an online
path-dependent cover realizes it. Achievability additionally assumes an ideal
pre-indexed query oracle; a raw archive may require more probes.

### Distributional and evolving-contract forms

For a distribution \(\mu\) on histories, define the mass analogue explicitly:

\[
\Gamma_{\mu,q}(C)
=
\max_{|Y|\le C}\mu\!\left(\bigcup_{y\in Y}A_y\right).
\]

The same deterministic fixed-budget transcript proof gives

\[
\Pr[\text{correct nonfallback}]
\le
\Gamma_{\mu,q}(2^{b+tw}).
\]

For randomized algorithms, conditioning on the coins gives a family of such
deterministic inequalities, but averaging the right-hand coverage curves is
not in general the same as substituting an expected transcript count. Expected
probe length, two-sided error, distributions over contract changes, and
amortized refinement therefore remain open model-specific extensions. This
report does **not** claim a proved relational rate-distortion law.

### Complete-justification verifier obstruction

Let the raw archive be cells \(D_1(h),\ldots,D_m(h)\), let \(s(h)\) be visible
state, and fix valid \((h,q,y)\). For every invalid \(h'\) with
\(s(h')=s(h)\),
define

\[
\Delta(h,h')=\{j:D_j(h)\ne D_j(h')\}.
\]

> **THEOREM V1.** In every perfectly sound **and complete** proof system, take
> a proof accepted for valid \((h,q,y)\). The verifier’s probe set on that
> accepted execution hits every \(\Delta(h,h')\). In a finite domain with
> unbounded proof and computation, the minimum accepted-proof probe count over
> complete sound systems equals the minimum \(J\) such that every
> **same-visible-state** archive agreeing with \(h\) on \(J\) is valid for
> \(y\).

The proof is an indistinguishability/reused-proof argument; the upper bound has
the proof name \(J\) and lets the verifier exhaustively validate the resulting
same-fiber cylinder. This is a finite **nonuniform, probe-only** upper bound:
the validity relation, verifier description, and exhaustive table computation
are uncharged. The implementation compares the hitting and cylinder
definitions for 525,348 valid-history cases through four Boolean cells.

For the all-zero claim over \(n\) raw bits in a constant visible-state fiber,
invalid alternatives \(e_i\) have singleton difference supports. Complete
negative evidence needs \(n\) probes;
the positive “some bit is one” claim needs one witness probe. This is a genuine
certificate/provenance-specific phenomenon that the transcript count misses,
but mathematically it is decision-tree certificate complexity. Nondeterministic
cell-probe verification is already explicit in Yin’s
[Cell-Probe Proofs](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-70575-8_7), and proof/stream
space tradeoffs appear in
[Annotations in Data Streams](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-02927-1_20).

There is no universal additive \(b+tw+P\) law. If the responder generates the
proof, \(P\) does not reveal archive information it never acquired. If an
external history-aware prover supplies \(P\), the model becomes
nondeterministic/Merlin–Arthur communication and requires a different soundness
definition.

## VIII. Exact non-toy reference system

The deterministic benchmark in
[`experiments/infinite-context-reference/2026-07-09`](../experiments/infinite-context-reference/2026-07-09/)
contains:

1. an immutable versioned archive;
2. a fixed query schema and initially compiled key set;
3. compiled complete-current state;
4. complete source-coverage and negative-evidence certificates;
5. a structural certificate checker and assertion gate;
6. exact full-replay and stable-key-index fallback;
7. causal checkpoints and a rolling exact window;
8. query-triggered stable-key refinement (named `counterexample_refined` in
   the executable, but not a general CEGAR learner);
9. a resource ledger complete for its declared logical counters, not total CPU
   or cryptographic work.

The 50-event workload contains most requested event-level features, including
revisions, retractions, contradictions, targeted invalidation, delayed keys,
exact wording, temporal queries, and negative evidence. It does not contain an
actual query-language extension or ingestion after a new-key obligation is
activated. Exact results:

Active bytes are canonical UTF-8 JSON encodings of each strategy's complete

versus 8,147 archive bytes under that fixed codec, charges 50 index writes and
24 cached-frontier-entry writes, and emits no unsupported assertion. The byte

This is an **EXACT benchmark**, not a preregistered empirical result. The design
bounds were frozen after implementation smoke testing. The independent oracle
replays raw events without production frontier helpers. The assertion gate
checks the answer/proof against the query boundary, source-universe digest,
archive digest, and a caller-supplied trusted active frontier; it does not by
itself bind that frontier to the archive. The independent replay comparison is
what detects semantic disagreement in this fixture. Archive immutability, index completeness,
source-universe identity, and active-state integrity remain trusted exact
primitives; no cryptographic membership or nonmembership proof is implemented.
The “new” obligations are new keys under an already fixed status/exact-source
query schema, not extensions to the query language. They are activated only
after the 50 events, so the fixture does not test continued ingestion or proof
invalidation after activation. Those limitations prevent it from supporting a
foundational positive-architecture claim.

## X. Strongest positive theorem

### Statement

For a fixed complete-frontier event system with \(m\) cells and \(v\) values
and immutable append-only publications (no targeted retroactive invalidation),
let a free exogenous schedule signal temporal obligations exactly at boundaries
\(0=t_0<\cdots<t_r=n\). Maintain one exact live segment frontier and freeze it
at each signal. Then:

1. the representation is exact and locally updateable;
2. it uses exactly (prod_jG_{m,v}(t_j-t_{j-1})) abstract states;
3. if \(r=o(n)\), active information is \(o(n)\);
4. each declaration uses zero archive probes and \(O(m)\) refinement work;
5. answers and complete frontiers have zero error;
6. adding an exact window of width \(w=o(n)\) supports any retroactive boundary
   in the window with at most \(w\) query-time replay; total active information
   with both components is
   \(O(r+r\log(1+n/r)+\log n+w)=o(n)\).

“Complete” here means equality to the maintained complete-frontier object. A
local verifier that does not trust that object is outside the theorem and may
need archive probes covered by V1.

### Classification

**STRONG SPECIALIZED THEOREM.** It directly answers a meaningful structured
evolving-contract class and is verified by two representations. It is not
foundational because it specializes classical temporal view maintenance, fixes
the ontology, assumes authenticated summaries, and has no matched lower bound
against a fixed external-memory/index model beyond the AB/BA obstruction.

Evidence that would move it higher:

- a natural broader syntactic extension class predicted by one structural
  parameter;
- a matching archive-probe/update/proof lower bound;
- a local binding complete-frontier certificate construction with all index
  writes/storage/verifier probes charged;
- a formal nonreduction against temporal view maintenance and authenticated
  data structures.

Evidence that would move it lower:

- a direct known theorem giving the same segment-product state formula and
  causal/rolling-window bounds;
- an implementation that relies on uncharged snapshots, hidden version chains,
  or a free completeness oracle.

## XI. Strongest negative theorem

### Statement

Relative to any visible active-state fiber and claimed complete output, the
minimum accepted-proof raw-archive probe complexity among perfectly sound and
complete finite nonuniform proof systems equals the minimum size of a cell set whose
observed cylinder, **intersected with that visible fiber**, contains no
falsifying history, when proof length and verifier computation are unbounded.
Hence an all-zero/complete-absence claim over \(n\) uncommitted raw cells whose
singleton flips remain in one visible fiber requires \(n\) probes, while an
existential positive claim has a one-cell witness.

Combined with view determinacy:

- if a new obligation is not determined by the old state, exact behavior must
  pay through refinement, archive access, fallback, or error;
- if it is determined, archive-free answering is semantically possible;
- for rich contract languages, deciding that boundary can itself be
  undecidable;
- even when the answer is determined, locally verifying complete provenance can
  require more access unless a binding summary/index has already changed the
  visible fiber.

### Classification

**CLASSICAL REFRAMING / NEGATIVE BOUNDARY.** It exposes a certificate-specific
cost missed by answer entropy in this repository's model, but its mathematical
skeleton is classical certificate/nondeterministic decision-tree complexity.
The value is diagnostic rather than a novelty claim.

Evidence that would move it higher:

- an online lower bound coupling this hitting geometry to update/refinement
  information transfer and higher-order certificate covers;
- a matching authenticated-index upper bound and lower bound in one fixed
  external-memory model;
- a separation not obtainable by a direct decision-tree, disjointness, cell-
  probe, or annotated-stream reduction.

Evidence that would move it lower:

- treating cryptographic commitments as information-theoretically collision
  free without stating the assumption;
- adding responder-generated proof bits to archive information capacity;
- applying the all-zero raw-cell bound after an exact aggregate/root was already
  retained and paid for.

## XII. Remaining path to Level 4 or Level 5

The next credible target is not another \(Q_{k,p}\) example. It is a matched
theorem for one natural dynamic provenance family combining three geometries:

\[
\text{higher-order accepted-output compatibility}
+
\text{counterexample-support hitting}
+
\text{online refinement information transfer}.
\]

A Level-4 candidate needs all of:

1. a contract language not defined circularly as “queries determined by the
   state”;
2. indefinite histories and a nontrivial evolving query/proof class;
3. zero answer and complete-justification error;
4. sublinear active information and sublinear amortized refinement/access for a
   positive regime;
5. a matched lower bound in the negative regime;
6. fixed word size, archive layout, preprocessing, persistent index storage,
   update writes, verifier model, and soundness assumption;
7. proof repair/invalidation accounting, not only new-answer state;
8. targeted specialist novelty review of the exact formulas and the combined
   theorem;
9. a theorem statement that remains nontrivial after every LLM term is removed.

The best concrete next family is **stable-key, dependency-bounded ontology
extension with version invalidation**:

- each event declares stable keys and bounded dependency edges at ingestion;
- an authenticated persistent index is charged on every update;
- extensions add local queries or invalidate source versions within dependency
  radius \(d\);
- proof repair follows reverse dependencies;
- the target is a matched bound in terms of postings touched, dependency
  boundary, verifier hitting number, and old-fiber split profile.

If that combined law reduces cleanly to view maintenance plus existing dynamic
cell-probe/annotated-stream results, the honest conclusion becomes
**CLASSICAL REFRAMING**. If it yields a new matched phase transition, it could
become a **FOUNDATIONAL CANDIDATE**. Level 5 would require broad uptake,
independent specialist validation, and evidence that the parameter governs
multiple natural families rather than one constructed benchmark.
