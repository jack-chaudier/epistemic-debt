Epistemic Debt

The answer–justification gap under context compression.

Jack GaffneyIndependent research · jack-chaudier

Repository Cite Licensing
Evidence ledger Road to durable context

Under memory pressure, systems keep correct answers after losing the reasons for them. The answers survive compression; the justifications don't. The difference is epistemic debt: a gap that can be hidden by answer accuracy and exposed when a task needs the missing reason. This program gives parts of the debt exact finite models and prices, then tests scoped predictions with preregistered experiments on frontier language models.

The practical edge: most answer-only evaluations measure whether an answer is right without checking whether its decision-relevant support survived. The justification gap, Δ = accuracy − justified accuracy, is measurable today with no access to model internals.

Prior & concurrent work: parts of this picture are anticipated — compression drops load-bearing content while accuracy holds (Łajewska et al. 2025), attribution drift (FACTUM 2026), attribution-QA metrics, and an independent real-document compression→decision-flip study (arXiv:2606.29251). What this program adds is the priced, within-item synthesis and the fixes; the full audit lives in the repo at theory/related-work-positioning.md. "Epistemic debt" is used here in the machine-internal justification-loss sense, distinct from the human-comprehension usage.

1Headline findings

When the evidence dies, models don't get noisy — they get predictable.

Destroy the deciding evidence and each model falls back to a characteristic default verdict — a learned section over the collapsed possibilities, not "unknown." One model's fingerprint, measured once on synthetic items, predicted the direction of 30/30 of its silent failures on real NTSB prose and 0.94 of its compression failures. The frozen one-fingerprint- per-model claim still failed as preregistered (1/3): another model flips sign with how the evidence died (surgical deletion → approve 0.81; lossy compression → deny). Companion probe: a student model trained on verdict-only traces recovered its "lost" math reasoning the moment CoT was forced (GSM8K 0.152 → 0.448) — the reasoning was latent; its fail-open miscalibration was not (0.997 → 0.953 under the same forcing, confabulating readings that don't exist). And offered an explicit "insufficient evidence" out, the two tested frontier readers take it 0.88–0.91 of the time — the deployable risk is systems that force the choice.

Preregistered · split verdicts, refutations included sections forced-cot

Two students, one teacher, one difference: what the loss paid for.

We distilled the same Qwen3-8B teacher into two 1.7B students — one trained on verdict-only traces, one on verdict+evidence traces — and ran both through the debt battery. The verdict-only student answers APPROVED on 99% of evidence-destroyed cases (Δ 0.98 vs 0.69, CI-separated) while looking better on the standard in-domain gauge; it also went silent — its GSM8K collapsed to 0.152 at 15 median output tokens while the justification-trained student matched stock weights (0.524 ≈ 0.516). Honest print: the preregistered parity precondition failed (gap 0.064 > 0.05), so the campaign verdict is VOID and every number here is exploratory — the parity-clean rerun is queued. The dissociation machinery itself worked end-to-end on open weights for $8.88 of GPU.

On semi-synthetic real prose, compressed "answers" can become vibes.

On 12 public-domain NTSB accident narratives with controlled policy readings injected into the prose, blind compaction dropped every witness and the surviving verdict was a content-free crash→deny reflex: decision accuracy on APPROVED documents was 0.00 for both models while DENIED-side accuracy looked fine (0.87–1.00). Justified accuracy exposed the failure instantly (J = 0.00–0.07); the fusion register recovered it to 0.67–0.77. This first real-prose tier passed with a load-bearing caveat: the source narratives are real, but the policy task is constructed; long-document budgets, untouched production transcripts, and broader domains remain open.

Preregistered · 4/4 both models realdoc

Answers survive compression; reasons don't.

After lossy summarization, models still deliver the correct verdict while naming the value that justifies it collapses below guessing — 0.93 vs 0.07 within the same items. The dissociation appears across four vendor families overall. A separate N=400 replication across three models produced confidence-interval half-widths of approximately 0.03–0.09, and a three-model domain battery tested six document schemas.

Preregistered highpower domains

In the transfer grid, reader changes rarely recover an explicitly lost witness.

Across nine compressor×reader pairings, direct recovery stayed low when the compacted artifact omitted the witness (≤ 0.107 lost vs ≥ 0.909 retained). Later constructed probes showed that all three readers can re-derive a missing witness by one arithmetic step, so the supported claim is narrower: audit both what the compactor preserves and what the reader can deterministically reconstruct.

Preregistered transfer grid

Checked finite models reveal a large design-versus-retrofit gap.

Across the checked finite settings, retrofitted abstention costs up to approximately 18× the debt. The largest ratio between that retrofit tax and the best designed-in honesty premium is 38.4×; one designed-in premium is 0.47× its debt. These are scoped extrema from distinct settings, not universal constants.

Witness loss is an event, not a rate: compaction debt is a settlement cost, not compound interest.

Iterated compaction at a fixed budget first looked like a ~7%/round tax (ρ̄ ≈ 0.93, below). Re-analysis and a preregistered length-clamped rerun re-identified it: with realized length held, witness survival over six further rounds decays 0.000 / 0.015 / 0.000 (three models; geometric model predicted 0.35/0.32/0.09). In a separate Grok schedule arm, a forced mid-chain budget squeeze concentrates loss in the drop round with flat survival afterward. Death was absorbing (0 resurrections in 849 transitions), matched-length iterated and direct survival differed by at most 0.042, and verdicts stayed flat. This is per-model evidence from three tested arms: the frozen panel-wide headline was inapplicable because its length guard failed 3/3.

Lost justification predicts future failure.

Change the policy after compression and re-query: accuracy is near-ceiling when the newly-required value survived in the artifact and collapses when it didn't — 0.978 vs 0.511 on the DENIED-source, original-accuracy-guard-passing subset. Debt is a brittleness forecast, not a bookkeeping curiosity.

Readers compute more than we predicted — our own law got refuted.

Candidate elimination is real, and arithmetic traces are suggestive, but the attempted c2 confirmation is VOID: a frozen surface-balance guard failed and was accidentally filtered. A larger-offset heuristic scores 46/60, above two model results. A balanced c3 rerun is required before claiming cross-model arithmetic recovery.

Static certificate compression can fail without bound online.

A compact representation that is valid now need not survive future updates. An exact unary family has static certificate minimum 2 but continuation-safe minimum n; allowing witness deletion can expand an add-only p+1-state system to all 2p survivor subsets.

Theorem + exact counterexamples continuation counterexamples

Future proof obligations—not history length alone—set active memory.

In an exact versioned contract, predeclared temporal checkpoints factor by interval. A fixed checkpoint count needs O(log n) active bits; positive-density checkpoints need Θ(n). Allowing even one adversarially selected past boundary makes every event history distinguishable.

Theorem + independent exact sweep checkpoint theorem

A one-sentence honesty rule drives unwitnessed confidence to zero.

Telling the compactor "never state an evaluative claim without its deciding value in the same clause" eliminated the mirage signature — confidently asserting a verdict while unable to name any reason — on all three models: 11–22% → 0.00. But not at matched budget: every model overrode the word limit to keep the values (~1.8×). The honesty rule dominates the length rule — compaction refuses to compress past the witness floor, converting hidden epistemic debt into visible token spend.

PreregisteredMatched-budget claim refuted signpost-fusion

Some measured debt is avoidable with a different compaction objective.

A one-line change to the compaction objective — spend the budget on names, numbers, and units — lifts justified accuracy 17/30 → 25/30 at the same length, and deterministic loss-ledger routing closes the rest (30/30 end-to-end in a cached structured-corpus prototype). These components are promising; an emitted, source-versioned runtime has not yet been built.

2The laws under test

Conjecture 1

Justification has no gradient. When an objective rewards answers without support, compression or training may preserve the answer channel while shedding decision-relevant evidence. The experiments establish scoped dissociations; the general optimization law remains a conjecture.

Revised account

Accounting identity and finite prices. Rate-1 conservation was refuted. In the checked finite layouts, debt, coverage loss, and honesty cost obey exact accounting relations; in the direct-retrieval grid, missing artifact support strongly constrained recovery. Candidate elimination and arithmetic reconstruction show that deployed debt is reader-relative. A general conservation law remains conjectural.

Pilot

Debt is brittleness. Systems carrying hidden debt fail disproportionately when conditions shift. Pilot confirmed the localization; the benchmark-parity kill-shot — distilled students matching teachers on evals while carrying invisible debt — is the open headline experiment.

Exact · finite

Calibrated abstention costs as much memory as justification — exactly verified for exact-witness calibration on all checked finite models, with a genuinely cheaper intermediate quotient discovered for certificate-level honesty. Confidence without calibration is cheap precisely because it carries no witness obligation.

3Method & reproducibility

Confirmatory runs are preregistered — predictions and thresholds fixed in the run spec before any API call — and every prediction is reported pass or fail, including the awkward ones. Exact results are exhaustive finite-model computations, stdlib-only, exiting 0 iff verified, pinned by regression tests. Three of the program's sharpest results are preregistered refutations of its own conjectures.

The road to durable, auditable context. The operational destination is a bounded working-context system that never silently asserts through missing decision-relevant support. For each consequential claim, the required witness is locally present, deterministically recovered from a named source version, or the claim is withheld with the missing obligation exposed.

Here, “infinite context” is a testable destination—not literal infinite tokens and not an achieved result. It means bounded active state over growing addressable evidence, evaluated at finite preregistered horizons and forced budget squeezes. Success requires stable justified answers without hiding failure behind universal abstention, falling answer accuracy, invalid source pointers, or unbounded retrieval cost.

1The proposed context contract

The design changes context from an unaudited text buffer into a bounded cache over named, versioned evidence. Each query creates support obligations. The runtime must resolve those obligations from the live working set or the source store before a consequential assertion can leave the system.

2Why the architecture is plausible

Finite models separate answer, certificate, and witness state. In one checked family, calibrated honesty via one valid certificate occupies an intermediate quotient between answer-only and exact-witness semantics. The same shortcut appears in three checked Q families, but not in the current causal_referee support model. This motivates a certificate layer; it does not prove language-level token savings.

Preregistered

Objectives can preserve more support

Value-dense compaction improved J at matched length. A same-clause fusion rule removed unwitnessed confidence in the tested arms, while visibly overspending the requested budget by roughly 1.8×.

VOID confirmation → constraint

Some losses may be reconstructible

Explicit arithmetic traces are suggestive, but the c2 confirmation failed a frozen surface-balance guard. Any verified-basis compressor needs a deterministic checker; model confidence and contaminated recovery rates are not proofs of reconstructibility.

Measured rounds 2–8

Loss tracks squeezing in the tested schedule arm

Held-length endpoint survival was 1.000, .985, and 1.000 across the three models, against frozen geometric predictions .651, .677, and .913. The panel guard failed 3/3, so no extrapolation beyond the measured horizon is shown. A separate Grok schedule arm localized loss at the forced squeeze.

Cached prototype

A loss manifest can route recovery

String-checked query-policy values on one cached structured corpus produced recall 1.00, precision .917, and 30/30 end-to-end recovery. Emitting and verifying that manifest live remains open.

3The evaluation contract

Δ alone cannot define success: a system that abstains on everything or lets answer accuracy collapse can also drive the gap toward zero. A durable-context runtime must report the following jointly, at each finite horizon and squeeze schedule.

Justified accuracy J

Correct answer with accepted decision-relevant support.

Answer accuracy

Preserves task performance instead of optimizing only the gap.

Coverage / abstention

Prevents abstain-on-everything from looking safe.

Unsupported assertion rate

The direct violation the runtime is meant to block.

Obligation recall

Does the extractor name all support required by the query?

Recovery recall

Can missing support actually be found when a valid source exists?

Source validity

Pointer, version, integrity, and availability at answer time.

Cost & stability

Tokens, calls, latency, horizon behavior, and squeeze recovery.

Certificate-compaction pilot. Run: certificates deliver the best justified accuracy on the DENIED side (J=.956–.978, Δ≤.044), but economy and APPROVED-side calibration claims split or failed. The finite quotient transfers only partially to generated language.

Stage 2

Verified-basis compaction. Test whether support can be omitted only when a deterministic checker reconstructs it from the retained basis. A depth ladder and hard derivation cap are prerequisites; a stochastic model's assertion that it “can re-derive” is not sufficient.

Squeeze–recovery benchmark. Compare a naive compaction stack with the emitted-ledger runtime across forced budget drops. Report J, answer accuracy, coverage, unsupported assertions, obligation/recovery recall, source validity, and cost—not Δ alone.

5Recursive systems: bounded returns as audit boundaries

Recursive Language Models offer a natural place to test this contract: each bounded sub-call return can be treated as a compaction boundary. The proposed return type is:

claim · witness or deterministic derivation · source pointer + version · known omissions · verification status

This is a conjectural integration. A vanilla recursive-system Δ audit and a certificate-typed return dataset are queued, not completed. The hypothesis is that typed returns turn recursive context management into an auditable chain of evidence obligations; the falsification test is whether they improve justified accuracy and unsupported-assertion rate at matched answer quality and cost.

The final evolution, if the open pieces survive testing, is not a model that literally holds everything in its token window. It is a model whose bounded working state remains answerable to a growing body of versioned evidence, and whose interface makes unsupported certainty harder than explicit uncertainty. The ambition is large; the claims remain finite, measured, and falsifiable.
